What are the spritual benefits of fasting?

In the our privious episode, we introduced the benefits of fasting and sincerely noted and promised that we would only eat when we feel like. Today we are going to talk about the spritual benefits of fasting. Since again this is a wide topic to exhaust in a single sitting, we shall discuss the role of fasting in managing corruption in the society.

We are all aware of how evil the curruption is. It is contagious disease, destroy societal trust, economic stability, and human rights. It exacerbates poverty, hinders development, and increases inequality by diverting resourses. It costs the global economy roughly $2 trillion dollars annualy.

Who want to be the agent of justice and fairness in the world full of corruption? Who want be a better leader for his family and society. This is only possible through self aware and contentment.

  • Fasting builds self-discipline: Corruption, which is mainly caused by greed can be fairly managed by fasting. It trains you to resist urges (like hunger, sex, etc), which strengthen self control. This same discipline can carry over into resisting temptations like bribery, dishonesty, or abuse of power.
  • Strengthens moral awareness: For both Islam and Christians fasting is tied to reflection, praye, and accountability. People become more concious of right and wrong, which discourage corrupt behavior.
  • Fasting encourages empathy: Experiencing hunger can make people more aware of others' struggles. This can reduce selfishness and greed, which are often drivers of corruption.
  • Fasting promotes integrity: Fasting is often done privately - no one knows if you 'cheat', like take a corner and eat something. Choosing to stay committed reinforces honesty and personal integrity, even when no one is watching.
  • Fasting reinforces social values:
    • When practiced collectively (like in communities and religious groups), fasting creates shared expectations of ethical behavior - discouraging corruption socially as well as personally.

Fasting doesn't eliminate corruption by itself. But it can shape character-buiding discipline, empathy and integrity - which are qualities that counteract corruption. Let us be fair today to ourselves, our families, our employees, our employers, and our co-workers!
